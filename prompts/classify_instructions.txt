When a publication could plausibly belong to more than one field, apply these priority rules:

1. Prefer the more specific field over a general one:
   - 'snow' over 'hydrology' if snowpack or cryospheric processes are central
   - 'groundwater' over 'hydrology' if subsurface storage and flow are central
   - 'fisheries biology' over 'aquatic ecology' if fish populations and management are central
   - 'geochemistry' over 'water quality' if element cycling, isotope tracing, or water-rock interaction across media are central

2. Prefer the physical science field over the engineering field when no infrastructure is designed, built, or operated:
   - 'hydrology' over 'water resource engineering' for process studies
   - 'water quality' over 'environmental engineering' for ambient condition studies without treatment system design or evaluation

3. Prefer 'aquatic toxicology' over 'water quality' when the central question is about contaminant effects on organisms.

4. Prefer 'restoration ecology' over 'aquatic ecology' or 'terrestrial ecology' when the primary question is about ecological recovery trajectories or the outcomes of restoration actions.

5. Prefer 'environmental DNA' or 'genomic DNA' over 'fisheries biology' or 'aquatic ecology' only when the method itself is the primary contribution — not when those tools are used to answer a biological or management question.

6. Prefer 'watershed management' over 'hydrology' when coordinated basin-scale governance and integrated management — not just hydrologic processes — are central.

7. Prefer 'climate risk and adaptation planning' over 'climatology' when risk assessment and adaptation planning, not climate characterization itself, are central.

8. Prefer 'natural resource economics' over 'environmental policy' when valuation, efficiency, or incentive-based analysis is central.

9. Prefer 'environmental justice' over 'environmental policy' when inequitable distribution of environmental burdens or benefits is the primary analytic frame.

When uncertain, ask: 'What is the main question this paper is trying to answer?' and select the field whose definition best matches that question.
